Menswear / Fashion History

This week’s Featured Book

The Men's Fashion Book

The first-ever authoritative A–Z celebration of the 500 greatest names in men’s fashion – an unprecedented guide to 200 years of men’s style through the work of designers, brands, photographers, icons, models, retailers, tailors, and stylists around the globe.

Find out more

Discover more in our collection

A History of Men's Fashion

Everything you ever wanted to know about men's clothing - and so much more - from the exact hour Nelson lost his right eye to the type of palm needed for a Panama hat, what Cary Grant’s tailor had to do to his shoulders - and those all-important questions of what to wear, when and why, including when to wear a bow tie (surely 'never' is the only answer?).

Find out more

Material Man

This volume examines masculine images in fashion and the media, attempting to answer the question What does it mean to be a man today?. It contains illustartions from films, television, magazines and the fine arts, along with scholarly essays.

Find out more

Men's Fashion Icons that Changed the World

Painfully cool and instantly recognisable, the 50 style trailblazers in this collection used fashion to attract attention, gain influence, express themselves and make powerful statements. From Fred Astaire to Prince, Marlon Brando to Bob Dylan, JFK to Fred Perry, this book covers the looks and stories of politicians and actors, musicians and designers.

Find out more

One Hundred Years of Menswear

A rich, comprehensive collection of images covering the revolution in menswear over the last 100 years with text by fashion historian Cally Blackman.
